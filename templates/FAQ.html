What is the NCAA Men's Division I Basketball Tournament?

It is the year-end championship for college basketball. 64 teams (well, technically 68) are invited, and it is a single elimination tournament taking place over a three-week period. You can read more about it on Wikipedia

Why should I participate?

Because it's the most fun time in sports. The drama is real and it's a major part of American sports culture. Also, because your friends and coworkers are doing it.

How much effort do I have to put into this?

As much as you like. It's pretty easy to choose 63 winners in whatever manner you like. Then, you can just watch the games (or not) and enjoy sharing the experience with your friends.

What are the chances of winning?

This is the most even competition ever. Everyone has an equal chance of winning.

How is the winner determined?

Your pool administrator will setup your individual pool with it's own scoring system.

Points are earned for each correct game winner chosen. Usually, later rounds are scored higher and bonus points are awarded for choosing lower seeds

Ok, but really, what should I know?

Well, here are a few tips.
  • No #16 seed has ever won a game
  • Every other seed has a chance, but lower numbers are better teams
  • Some people love to pick based on team name, mascot or location and that can work!
  • Last year, a randomly generated bracket won the pool

That's useful info, but why are you so excited about this?

We are nerds. So, here's some numbers:
  • There are 63 games (we don't count "The First Four")
  • That means there are 263 = 9.223 x 1018 = 9,223,372,036,854,780,000 possible outcomes
  • That's a lot of outcomes
  • You can (and we do) spend all day analyzing these outcomes
  • Though the teams are ranked, lower-ranked teams win very frequently

What else can I learn about this?

If that's not enough info for you, here's a few articles that you might like:
  • How to Win Your NCAA Pool (slate.com)
  • When Losing Leads to Winning (freakonomics.com)
  • March Madness: Bracket-Picking 101 (huffingtonpost.com)

This is awesome. What will I be able to see?

You can analyze your pool to scary levels of depth. For example:
  • We'll give you ways to see how many games people have picked with you
  • We'll identify games that are important to you and who is on your side
  • We'll show you the games that matter to the outcome of your pool
If what we give you isn't enough and you ask nicely, we can get you an export of all the picks in your pool to run your own analysis.

Please share your new analysis with us so we can build it for everyone
